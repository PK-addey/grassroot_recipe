Palava sauce

Description

Palava is a word coined from the English word palaver which in turn is from a Portuguese word that means idle chatter. However this word became the name of a popular West African delicacy, no one knows. But the meaning of the name suggests that the delicious taste of the dish brought with it idle chatter by the people who ate it.

This dish cannot be originally linked to any West African country. In Ghana, palava sauce is prepare with kontonmire leaves and agushie(melon seeds) as the main ingredients. Palava sauce can be served with any dish of choice some of which are cooked rice, yam and plantain.

Ingredients

5 Servings
  • Kontomire leaves/ cocoyam leaves About 15
  • 5 small tomatoes
  • 3 medium sized onions
  • Pepper
  • 1 large smoked salmon
  • 1 cup of grounded melon seeds(agushie or akatua)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 Salted fish (Koobi)
  • 3 tsp of shrimp powder
  • Salt
  • Spices
